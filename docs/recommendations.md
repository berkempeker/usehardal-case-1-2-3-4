# Recommendations

### Priority 1: Win the Comparison Game (Month 1-2)

**Problem:** When prospects Google "Stape alternative" or "Jentis vs Stape," Hardal doesn't show up.

- Create 5-7 **comparison landing pages**: Hardal vs Stape, vs Jentis, vs Addingwell, vs Taggrs, vs DIY sGTM
- Focus on honest, data-backed differentiators (end-to-end platform vs. just hosting, Turkish/EU data residency, pricing)
- Create a "Why Hardal" page consolidating competitive positioning
- Reach out to comparison sites (SaaSHub, G2, OMR Reviews) to ensure accurate, updated listings

### Priority 2: Double Down on Shopify (Month 1-3)

**Problem:** 6 reviews on 5.0 stars — massive untapped channel.

- The Shopify App Store is a **free distribution channel** with built-in purchase intent
- Run a campaign to get every existing Shopify customer to leave a review (target: 25+ reviews)
- Create Shopify-specific content: "Shopify server-side tracking guide," "Fix Shopify checkout data loss," "Shopify Meta CAPI setup"
- Highest-ROI move — Shopify merchants actively search for conversion tracking solutions

### Priority 3: Build a LinkedIn Content Engine (Month 1-ongoing)

**Problem:** Founders and team have almost zero visible presence on LinkedIn, where every marketing/analytics decision-maker lives.

- Founders should post 3-4x/week: data privacy hot takes, customer wins, behind-the-scenes metrics from open dashboard, "myth vs reality" of server-side tracking
- Repurpose every case study into a LinkedIn narrative post
- Comment strategically on posts from Google Analytics, Meta ads, and MarTech influencers
- Costs $0 — single most effective B2B awareness channel in 2026

### Priority 4: Programmatic SEO — Industry & Platform Pages (Month 2-4)

**Problem:** Content targets developers but not the economic buyers searching for solutions.

- Create templatized landing pages:
  - **By industry:** "Server-side tracking for e-commerce / travel / gaming / fintech"
  - **By platform:** "Meta CAPI setup service," "TikTok eAPI implementation," "GA4 server-side migration"
  - **By problem:** "Fix iOS data loss," "Ad blocker tracking recovery," "GDPR-compliant analytics"
- Each page should include relevant case study data and a CTA to book a demo

### Priority 5: Get Serious About G2 and Review Sites (Month 2-3)

**Problem:** Thin presence means prospects can't validate Hardal through third-party trust signals.

- Run a structured review campaign targeting all 82 customers
- Target G2, Capterra, Shopify App Store, and Product Hunt
- Reviews compound — they improve SEO, conversion rates, and feed into analyst reports
- Goal: 30+ G2 reviews within 3 months

### Priority 6: Strategic Content Partnerships (Month 3-5)

- **Guest posts** on Measure School, Analytics Mania, Simo Ahava's blog — the GTM/analytics community reads these religiously
- **Webinars** with complementary tools (Adjust, Appsflyer, CMS platforms)
- **Sponsor or speak** at MeasureCamp, SuperWeek, or Marketing Analytics Summit
- Get listed in every "top server-side tracking tools" article — most currently omit Hardal entirely

### Priority 7: Expand Geographically with Localized Content (Month 4-6)

**Problem:** Strong in Turkey but barely visible internationally.

- Turkish customer base is a strength, but the $750K ARR ceiling will hit fast in Turkey alone
- Create English-first content targeting EU (GDPR angle) and US (iOS/cookie deprecation angle)
- Consider German and French content — GDPR sensitivity is highest in DACH and France, competitors like Jentis own that narrative
- The EU data residency angle (multi-cloud including Huawei) is undermarketed

## Bottom Line

Hardal has a strong product with impressive growth (310% YoY) and real enterprise customers — but the marketing is almost entirely **sales-driven**. For a 12-person team at $750K ARR, that's worked well. But to break past $2M ARR and compete internationally with Stape (which has far more brand awareness), Hardal needs to shift from "sell to each customer 1:1" to "be found by customers who are already looking."

**The three highest-leverage moves that cost almost nothing:**

1. **Comparison pages** (capture existing demand)
2. **LinkedIn founder content** (build brand awareness)
3. **Shopify review campaign** (compound social proof)

Everything else builds on these foundations.
